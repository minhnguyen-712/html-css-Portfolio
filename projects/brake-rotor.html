Brake Rotor Redesign

Problem: While the team's previous brake rotor design was lightweight and successfully reduced the car's overall mass, it proved to be too vulnerable during testing. The aggressive geometry was not stiff enough to survive the extreme heat and braking forces from the calipers. This caused the rotor to flex and warp, leading to inconsistent braking
My Contribution: I was involved in the redesign of a new, more robust rotor using 4140 Steel. My main role was to perform FEA (Finite Element Analysis) in SolidWorks to prove the new design's strength and stiffness before it was manufactured.

Front Rotor Static FEA

Strength: Achieved a 2.37x Factor of Safety. (The max stress was 1.749e+08 N/m², well below the 4.150e+08 N/m² yield strength).

Stiffness: Showed a maximum deflection of only 0.013 mm

Rear Rotor Static FEA

Strength: Achieved a 5.43x Factor of Safety. (The max stress was 7.637e+07 N/m², well below the 4.150e+08 N/m² yield strength).

Stiffness: Showed a maximum deflection of only 0.006 mm (5.880e-03 mm).

Result

The new rotor design was manufactured and validated on the car over multiple test days and at the Formula Wheat competition. It proved to be stiff and reliable, passing all 4-wheel lockup brake tests and giving our drivers consistent, dependable braking.
