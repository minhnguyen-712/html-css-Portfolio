Formula SAE Braking System Design

SolidWorks CAD/CAE Material Science

Project Overview

Designed and analyzed a complete braking system for the WashU Racing Formula SAE vehicle. The project focused on optimizing stopping power while maintaining reliability and meeting competition regulations.

Key Responsibilities

  • Led the design and analysis of brake rotors and calipers
  • Performed thermal and mechanical stress simulations
  • Selected materials based on performance requirements
  • Integrated the system with the vehicle's pedal box

Technical Details

The braking system design incorporated several critical components:

  • Dual-piston floating calipers for optimal pad contact
  • Ventilated rotors with computational fluid dynamics (CFD) validated cooling
  • Adjustable brake bias system
  • Custom mounting brackets designed for minimal flex

Results & Performance

  • Achieved 20% reduction in stopping distance
  • Improved thermal efficiency by 30%
  • Reduced overall system weight by 15%
  • Met all FSAE safety requirements with margin

Tools & Software Used

  • SolidWorks for 3D modeling and assembly
  • ANSYS for FEA and thermal analysis
  • MATLAB for performance calculations
  • Data acquisition system for testing
